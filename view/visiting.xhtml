Visiting Boston

Things to Do, Places to See

Boston Common and Public Gardens
Park Street
Description: These two national historic landmarks are central public parks in the city. While visiting the Public Garden, try a relaxing ride on the Swan Boats.

Boston Harbor Islands
Boston Harbor, Long Wharf
(617) 223-8666
Description: Join National Park Rangers and scientists on board a Boston Harbor Cruise boat to explore the lighthouses, forts, and natural wonders of this 34-island park area.

Faneuil Hall
1 Faneuil Hall
Description: Faneuil Hall has served as a marketplace and a meeting hall since 1742 and is part of the Freedom Trail. Its marketplace is a popular shopping and dining destination.

Newbury Street
Description: Eight blocks filled with salons, boutiques, and fabulous dining. This street is one of the best areas to shop.

Shops at Prudential & Copley Place
Description: Two large shopping centers connected by a bridge, filled with dining and a wide range of shops.

Fenway Park
4 Yawkey Way
(617) 226-6000
Description: Home of the World Series ‘04 and ‘07 champs, the Boston Red Sox. Tours of the park are available.

The New England Aquarium
1 Central Wharf
(617) 973-5200
Description: Experience more than 70 exhibits,
featuring animals from around the world.

Prudential Tower SkyWalk
The Prudential Tower
(617) 859-0648
Description: Boston's only sky-high vantage point for sweeping 360 degree views of Greater Boston and beyond.

Boston Duck Tours
Museum of Science, The Prudential Center
(617) 267-DUCK
Description: Tour the sights of Boston on a fleet of "Ducks", amphibious vehicles which provide narrated, half-land, half-water tours.

Wally's Jazz Café
427 Massachusetts Avenue
617-424-1408
Description: This jazz club is among the oldest family owned and operated club in existence and features live music 365 days a year.

Harpoon Brewery
Seaport
Description: Visit New England's largest craft brewery.

Freedom Trail
National Park Visitor Center, 15 State Street
(617) 242-5642
Description: Free 90 minute walking tour led by National Park Service Rangers along the heart of Boston's Freedom Trail. Discover Boston's role in the American Revolution.

Boston Symphony Orchestra
301 Massachusetts Avenue
(617) 266-1492
Description: Experience the excitement of the Boston Pops with the sounds of Broadway, jazz, rock and roll, classical, or traditional Pops favorites, and find out why it's called "America's Orchestra."

Museum of Science
1 Science Park
(617) 589-0300
Description: Features a revolving schedule of Imax films, temporary exhibitions and planetarium shows all based on science. Very educational and fun!

Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum
280 the Fenway
(617) 566-1401
Description: Preeminent collection contains more than 2500 paintings, sculptures, tapestries, furniture, and decorative arts from cultures spanning thirty centuries.

Museum of Fine Arts
465 Huntington Avenue
Description: See a collection of over 450,000 objects, some of which are the most rare and important artistic treasures in the world.
